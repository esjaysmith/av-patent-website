## Writing Tone & Style

**Tone:** Authoritative and strategic, competitive but not aggressive, respectful of Tesla's achievements while positioning our solution

**Voice:** Second person ("you", "your company") to create direct connection with competitive decision-makers

**Style Guidelines:**
- **Strategic Language:** Emphasize competitive positioning, not attacking Tesla
- **Data-Driven:** Use specific numbers, timelines, costs (credibility for engineering/business leaders)
- **Respect Competition:** Acknowledge Tesla's success (builds credibility, not dismissive)
- **Action-Oriented:** Every section should lead to clear strategic action
- **Technical Depth:** Balance technical accuracy with business strategy (dual audience: engineering + C-suite)

**Avoid:**
- Tesla-bashing or dismissive language (unprofessional, undermines credibility)
- Overpromising ("Beat Tesla with this one patent!" - unrealistic)
- Defensive tone ("We're just as good as Tesla" - sounds weak)
- Legal advice ("You must license this patent" - suggest consulting patent attorney)
- Hype language ("Revolutionary patent!" - maintain professional tone)

**Competitive Positioning Language Examples:**

**Good:**
- "Tesla's unsupervised FSD validates camera-first approach—competitors need camera-based patents to compete credibly"
- "Our dual-module safety architecture provides differentiation from Tesla's single-module approach"
- "Strategic patent licensing enables competitive positioning without Tesla's 10-year development timeline"

**Bad (Avoid):**
- "Tesla's patents are weak—our patent is better" (unprofessional, dismissive)
- "You'll beat Tesla with this patent" (overpromising, unrealistic)
- "Tesla is overrated—camera-first isn't that hard" (undermines own argument)
