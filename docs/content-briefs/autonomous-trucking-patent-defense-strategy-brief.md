## Writing Guidelines

### Tone & Voice
- **Authoritative but approachable**: Expert guidance without jargon overload
- **Direct and action-oriented**: Clear next steps, no fluff
- **Data-driven**: Support claims with specific numbers, dates, sources
- **Respectful of audience expertise**: CTOs and patent managers are technical—don't oversimplify

### Sentence Structure
- **Average sentence length**: 15-20 words
- **Paragraph length**: 2-4 sentences maximum (scannable)
- **Active voice preferred**: "License camera-based patents" (not "Camera-based patents can be licensed")
- **Avoid jargon without explanation**: Define technical terms on first use

### Content Flow
1. **Hook with data**: Start each section with compelling statistic or example
2. **Problem → Solution**: Identify pain point, then present patent licensing as solution
3. **Support with evidence**: Every claim backed by source (Tier 1 or Tier 2)
4. **CTA within reach**: Keep call-to-action visible (never more than 2 scrolls away)

### SEO Writing Best Practices
- **Primary keyword density**: 1-2% (target: 18-44 uses in 1,800-2,200 words)
- **Secondary keywords**: Natural distribution throughout (1-2 uses each)
- **LSI keywords**: Related terms for semantic relevance (trucking, commercial vehicles, fleet, freight, Class 8, long-haul, logistics, OEM, camera-based, visual navigation, safety systems)
- **Questions as H3 headings**: Use long-tail question keywords where appropriate
- **Bold key phrases**: Help readers scan (but don't overuse)
